🔐 PhotoVault — Private Photo Locker

PhotoVault

Last updated: March 27, 2026

Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how PhotoVault — Private Photo Locker (“PhotoVault,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) treats information when you use our iOS app. PhotoVault is a private locker for your photos with a four-digit passcode, Face ID or Touch ID unlock, an optional decoy password that shows an empty vault, and intrusion detection that logs failed unlock attempts. Your media stays on your device.

Data Collection

We do not collect any personal data. PhotoVault does not upload your photos, passcodes, biometric templates, album names, or intrusion logs to our servers. We do not operate a cloud backend for storing your vault contents. There is no user account, no analytics pipeline, and no advertising SDK receiving your data.

GDPR: We do not collect or process personal data on our systems. All photos and vault data reside exclusively on your device within the app’s sandboxed local storage. Processing (including encryption at rest as implemented by the app and iOS) happens on-device. We cannot access your vault contents.

On-Device Processing

Imported photos are stored in the app’s protected sandbox on your iPhone or iPad. Biometric unlock uses Apple’s LocalAuthentication framework: Face ID and Touch ID are handled by the system secure enclave; we do not receive raw biometric data. Decoy password and main vault behaviors are enforced locally. Intrusion-related information (such as failed attempt counts) is recorded only on your device as designed by the app.

Permissions Used

  • Photo Library — Lets you import photos into the vault and export photos back to your library when you choose. PhotoVault does not sync your library to our servers.
  • Face ID / Touch ID — Optional biometric unlock via Apple’s LocalAuthentication API. You can use a passcode instead if you prefer. Apple’s system dialogs explain how biometrics work on your device.

Third-Party Services

PhotoVault does not integrate third-party analytics, advertising, or cloud backup services that would receive your vault data. We do not sell or rent your information.

In-App Purchase

Pro features (unlimited photos and albums, Face ID/Touch ID integration as offered in Pro, decoy password, intrusion alerts) are available as a one-time purchase (USD $2.99, or local equivalent). Apple processes payment; we do not receive your full card details.

The free tier allows up to 20 photos and 3 albums.

Children’s Privacy

PhotoVault is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. The app does not transmit vault contents to us.

International Data Transfers

We do not host your photos or passcodes. No vault data is transferred internationally by us because it does not leave your device for processing on our systems. If you use iCloud or other Apple features outside the app, those are governed by Apple’s terms and your settings.

GDPR Compliance (EEA, UK, and Similar Laws)

For users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, and similar jurisdictions: No personal data is collected or processed by us for the core vault functionality; all such data remains exclusively on your device. We act neither as a recipient nor as a processor of your photos on our own infrastructure. Rights such as access, erasure, or portability for data stored only on your device are exercised by you locally (for example, by deleting items in the app or removing the app, subject to your own backups). Purchase records may be held by Apple under its policies. Contact us at the email below for questions about this Privacy Policy.

CCPA / CPRA Compliance (California)

California residents: We do not sell or share personal information as defined under the CCPA/CPRA through PhotoVault. We do not collect personal information categories from the app for our own databases. Data you keep in the vault is not transmitted to us. For information related to Apple purchases, refer to Apple’s California privacy notices.

Analytics

We do not use in-app analytics or cross-app tracking in PhotoVault.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy. The date at the top will reflect the latest revision. Significant changes may be summarized in App Store release notes.
