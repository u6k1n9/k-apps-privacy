📄 ScanOnce — Document Scanner

ScanOnce

Last updated: March 27, 2026

Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how ScanOnce — Document Scanner (“ScanOnce,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) handles information when you use our iOS application. ScanOnce is a privacy-first document scanner with on-device OCR in seven languages, PDF export, and automatic edge detection. We designed the app so that your documents stay on your device.

Data Collection

We do not collect any personal data. ScanOnce does not gather, store, transmit, or sell your name, email address, document contents, scan images, OCR text, usage logs, device identifiers, or analytics data. The app makes zero network requests for scanning, OCR, or core functionality. There is no account system and no server-side processing of your scans.

On-Device Processing

All scanning, edge detection, optical character recognition (OCR), and PDF generation run entirely on your iPhone or iPad using Apple’s on-device technologies, including the Apple Vision framework. Your documents are processed in local memory and sandboxed app storage controlled by iOS. Nothing is sent to our servers because we do not operate backend services for these features.

Permissions Used

  • Camera — Required to capture new scans of paper documents. Images are processed on-device and saved only where you choose (for example, to your photo library or within exported files).
  • Photo Library — Used to import existing images for scanning and to save exported documents or PDFs when you explicitly choose to do so. Access is limited to what you authorize in iOS Settings.

Third-Party Services

ScanOnce does not integrate third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or cloud storage SDKs that would receive your document data. We do not share your information with data brokers or marketing partners.

In-App Purchase

Optional Pro features (unlimited scans, OCR, PDF export, batch scanning, and all filters) are available as a one-time purchase (USD $3.99, or your local equivalent). Payments are processed exclusively by Apple. We do not receive your full payment card number. Apple may process transaction data under its own privacy policy.

The free tier allows three scans so you can try the app before upgrading.

Children’s Privacy

ScanOnce is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children, because the app does not collect personal data at all.

International Data Transfers

Because we do not collect or host your scan data on our systems, no document or OCR data leaves your device through our app for processing by us. Any export you perform (for example, saving a PDF to Files or Photos) is under your control and subject to Apple’s and your chosen services’ policies.

GDPR Compliance (EEA, UK, and Similar Laws)

If you are in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or another jurisdiction with similar data protection laws: ScanOnce does not process personal data on our behalf as a data controller for the core app experience—we do not collect identifiers or document content. Processing of any data that remains only on your device is under your control as the user. For purchase-related information, Apple acts as the merchant. You may exercise rights related to Apple-held data through Apple. For questions about this policy, contact us at the email below.

CCPA / CPRA Compliance (California)

California residents: We do not sell or share personal information as defined by the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), and we do not collect the categories of personal information described in those laws through ScanOnce for our own use. Because the app does not transmit your scans or OCR results to us, we have no such data to disclose, delete, or correct on our systems. In-app purchases are handled by Apple; see Apple’s California privacy disclosures for payment-related data.

Analytics

We do not use analytics, tracking, or fingerprinting tools in ScanOnce.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when we do. Material changes may also be noted in the App Store update notes. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact
